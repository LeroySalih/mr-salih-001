    1.2 Flowcharts

    LO:
    • To be able to interpret a given flowchart.
    • To be able to complete a trace table associated with a flowchart.

    Pre Lesson Videos

    Using bullet points, describe in your exercise book how you complete a trace table.

    Key Terms

    The following terms are introduced: flowchart, Start/End, Instruction, Decision

    Key Notes

    For those who took iGCSE Computer Science, note the simplified symbols used in Decision (i.e. output, etc).

    1.3 Bubble sort

    LO:
    • To be able to carry out a bubble sort.

    Pre Lesson Videos

    Note: This video demonstrates a comparison by comparison approach for the first pass, and then a pass by pass approach for the remaining passes.

    The following terms are introduced: Bubble sort, comparison, pass, swap

    Key Notes

    Ensure that you understand what you are being asked to show: comparison by comparison, pass by pass, first line or all numbers.

    1.4 Quick sort

    LO:
    • To be able to carry out a quick sort.

    Pre Lesson Videos

    Using bullet points, describe in your exercise book how you conduct a quick sort.

    Key Terms

    The following terms are introduced: Quick sort, middle number, pivot

    Key Notes

    • Ensure that you understand how to calculate the middle number, as this will become the pivot.
    • Ensure that you are clear in the use of current and previous pivot symbols.

    1.5 Bin Packing Algorithms

    LO:
    • To be able to carry out 3 bin packing algorithms and understand their strengths.
    • To be able to understand how to calculate the lower bound of the bins required.

    Pre Lesson Videos

    Using bullet points, describe in your exercise book how you complete the three bin filling algorithms.

    Key Terms

    The following terms are introduced: first-fit, first-fit decreasing, full-bin

    Key Notes

    • Ensure that you are utilising the correct algorithm.
    • Ensure that you understand how to calculate the lower bound of the number of bins
    • Ensure that you understand the rules for calculating wasted space.
